Transparency & Public Auditability

CCU is designed so that issuance is explainable and externally reviewable. Ledger files, anchor roots, emergency policy bounds, and role-based approvals form the public integrity layer.

Public Transparency Commitments

Area Public Commitment
Ledger Files Operational and emergency ledgers are prepared as auditable records.
Anchor Roots Ledger roots are anchored on-chain to prevent silent rewriting.
Role Logic Issuance depends on explicit governance roles and approvals.
Emergency Policy Emergency issuance follows floor, ceiling, and hard cap rules.
Treasury Support Needs beyond emergency mint range should be handled by treasury support logic.

Anchor Model

Ledger entries are reduced to a root so that on-chain records can verify the existence of the public issuance basis without requiring the full document to be stored on-chain.

  • Ledger events are normalized
  • Leaf hashes are generated
  • Merkle root is calculated
  • Root is anchored on-chain

Reporting Philosophy

CCU does not aim for vague issuance. The goal is to show what was approved, what was minted, and what remains under treasury or emergency policy constraints.

What Users Should Be Able to Verify

  • That a ledger file existed before issuance
  • That the anchored root matches the published ledger basis
  • That the mint path followed policy bounds
  • That treasury and operations functions remain separated
  • That emergency actions were not arbitrary or unlimited
Transparency is not only publishing numbers. It is making issuance behavior reviewable.
