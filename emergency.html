Emergency Issuance Policy

CCU emergency issuance is not intended to behave like normal daily minting. It is a bounded, policy-driven response layer designed for urgent needs while reducing the risk of uncontrolled market shock.

500,000
Emergency Floor

If the verified emergency need is below this level, the minimum emergency issuance is still set at 500,000 CCU.

1,200,000
Emergency Normal Ceiling

If the verified need is between the floor and this range, the emergency mint follows the verified amount. Above this range, the mint is capped here.

5,000,000
Emergency Hard Cap

This is the maximum policy bound for emergency capacity. It is not intended for arbitrary, uncontrolled issuance.

Emergency Decision Logic

  • If need < 500,000: emergency mint = 500,000
  • If 500,000 ≤ need ≤ 1,200,000: emergency mint = verified need
  • If need > 1,200,000: emergency mint = 1,200,000
  • Remaining unmet need: should be handled by treasury support policy

This structure exists so that emergency issuance remains meaningful in crisis conditions while avoiding tiny or market-disturbing arbitrary outputs.

Emergency Operational Flow

1. Evidence

Crisis evidence and ledger basis are prepared.

2. Approval

Admin, operator, verifier, and auditor participate in the activation process.

3. Policy Mint

Mint follows floor/ceiling policy, not tiny event fragments.

4. Treasury Backstop

Amounts beyond normal emergency mint are treasury candidates.

Why This Matters

A crisis-response system that mints only trivial amounts during real emergency conditions fails its own purpose. CCU emergency policy aims to preserve minimum meaningful response while still respecting issuance bounds and market caution.

At the same time, if a crisis is extremely large, the system should not force unlimited on-chain minting. That is where treasury reserve support becomes the correct response layer.

Emergency policy is bounded, evidence-based, and designed to reduce both operational paralysis and market panic.
