Protocol Layer

Verified Compensation Credit System

CCU is a verified compensation-credit protocol designed for auditable regeneration, harm prevention, emergency response, and policy-based issuance. The protocol combines anchored ledger files, role-based approvals, and issuance controls so that minting is explainable, reviewable, and limited by explicit governance rules.

Core Model
PoC + Dual Emission

Proof-based issuance with treasury reserve and operational emission.

Network
Polygon Mainnet

Low-cost, transparent, and wallet-friendly infrastructure.

Protocol Goals

  • Transparent issuance with anchored ledger evidence
  • Emergency response with explicit floor, ceiling, and hard cap
  • Separation between treasury reserves and daily operations
  • Multi-role governance for operational integrity
  • Public auditability through published monthly files

Architecture

The protocol uses a token contract, an issuer contract, and a policy layer for daily issuance, treasury reserve logic, and emergency issuance. Ledger files are anchored on-chain and issuance actions are tied to role-based approvals.

  • CCUToken: ERC-20 compatible token contract
  • CCUIssuer: issuance logic, month/period state, anchoring, emergency actions
  • Treasury: long-term reserve and backstop source
  • Operations Wallet: daily and emergency active-use wallet

Governance Roles

  • Admin / Deployer: setup, activation, policy execution, role updates
  • Operator: operational approval layer
  • Verifier: evidence and validity approval layer
  • Auditor: oversight and audit approval layer

Roles are address-based. If a participant must be replaced, their address can be removed from the role and a new address assigned.

Issuance Channels

  • Genesis Mint: one-time reserve mint to treasury
  • Daily Mint: operational issuance for normal programs
  • Emergency Mint: policy-bounded issuance for crisis response
  • Treasury Support: reserve-backed support for needs beyond emergency mint range

Period Logic

The protocol can operate with month keys or more granular period keys. That makes it possible to support multiple operational windows without losing audit structure.

  • Monthly or multi-batch period anchoring
  • Evidence-linked issuance events
  • Emergency flow separated from normal operational flow

Operational Flow

Step 1

Ledger Preparation

Events, evidence, region, and period information are prepared in ledger files.

Step 2

Anchor

The ledger root is anchored so the issuance basis becomes publicly auditable.

Step 3

Role Approval

Operator, verifier, and auditor participate in signed or on-chain approvals.

Step 4

Mint Execution

Mint occurs according to policy rules, role approvals, and anchored evidence.

CCU Protocol is designed for transparent, bounded, and auditable issuance.
