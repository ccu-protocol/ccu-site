Verified Compensation Credits

CCU is a verified compensation credit protocol based on a Proof-of-Compensation model with a dual-emission structure. It is designed for transparent, auditable recovery, prevention, and humanitarian support programs.

CCU is designed as a humanitarian compensation protocol. Use of the protocol for military operations, weapons programs, or combat financing is strictly prohibited. At the same time, support eligibility is based on verified human harm rather than profession, which means individuals affected by conflict, disaster, or policy failure may be considered regardless of occupation.

Humanitarian Neutrality

CCU is designed to address verified human harm through transparent, auditable, and non-political compensation logic.

The protocol does not serve political agendas, military operations, or state power competition. Its purpose is to support verified humanitarian recovery, prevention, and compensation capacity with respect for human dignity and clear policy boundaries.
